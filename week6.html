Week 6: Portable Table Assembly

Date: November 4 - 10, 2025

  • Skills Learned: Leg design, interlocking slots, precision cutting
  • Digital Tools and Software Used: CAD software, laser cutting, precision measurement
  • Topics: Leg assembly, geometric stability, compact design
  • Key Concepts: Slot integration, V-geometry, weight distribution
  • Challenges Faced: Slot precision, leg angle optimization, stability testing

Introduction

This week's project focused on designing and assembling a compact 3-part portable table featuring innovative V-shaped legs that cross to form an X-shaped support structure. The unique leg design provides exceptional stability while maintaining minimal weight and compact storage capabilities.

The table consists of three precisely engineered components: two identical legs and one circular tabletop. Each V-leg features a 6mm thick profile that tapers from 45mm at the top to 90mm at the base, with a precisely cut 6mm slot running through the center for interlocking assembly.

Key Design Features

  • V-Shaped Geometry: Legs taper from 90mm at base to 45mm at top for optimal stability
  • Center Slot System: 6mm wide slot running through leg center enables interlocking assembly
  • X-Formation: Legs cross at 90-degree angle when assembled, creating inherent structural stability
  • Material Efficiency: 6mm thickness optimized for strength-to-weight ratio
  • Tool-Free Assembly: No additional fasteners required - components interlock securely

Assembly Sequence

Step 1: Leg Interlocking

Take both V-shaped legs and align the 6mm center slots. Slide one leg through the slot of the other at a 90-degree angle. The legs will naturally form an X-shape due to the V-geometry and slot alignment.

Step 2: X-Formation Lock

Continue sliding until the legs are fully engaged. The V-shape ensures the legs lock securely at the crossing point. The wide 90mm base provides immediate stability.

Step 3: Tabletop Placement

Place the 90mm circular tabletop onto the X-shaped base. The top will rest securely on the 45mm wide upper sections of the V-legs. The frosted surface provides grip.

Step 4: Stability Verification

Test table stability by applying gentle pressure. The X-formation combined with the V-geometry creates a remarkably stable structure despite the minimal component count.

Reflection

Design Innovations

The X-shaped V-leg table introduced several innovative design solutions:

  • Geometric Stability: V-shape combined with X-formation creates inherent structural integrity without additional fasteners
  • Slot Precision: 6mm slot with ±0.1mm tolerance ensures perfect interlocking and smooth assembly
  • Weight Optimization: Total weight of only 320g while maintaining structural strength
  • Rapid Assembly: Three-part design assembles in under 10 seconds without tools
  • Compact Storage: Flat-packed components occupy minimal space - ideal for portable applications

Technical Challenges & Solutions

Several technical challenges were successfully addressed during the design and manufacturing process:

  • Slot Width Optimization: 6mm chosen as optimal balance between structural integrity and assembly ease
  • Material Selection: Acrylic provided ideal combination of rigidity, weight, and manufacturing precision
  • Geometric Calculations: V-angle carefully calculated to maximize stability while minimizing material usage
  • Manufacturing Precision: Laser cutting achieved required ±0.1mm tolerances for perfect slot fit

Learning Outcomes

This project provided valuable insights into several key areas:

  • Geometric Design Principles: Understanding how simple shapes can create complex, stable structures
  • Precision Manufacturing: Importance of tight tolerances in interlocking designs
  • Material Properties: How material thickness and properties affect structural performance
  • User Experience: Designing for both assembly and disassembly considerations
  • Problem Solving: Iterative approach to optimizing slot dimensions and leg angles

Future Applications & Improvements

This design approach has potential for various applications and improvements:

  • Scalable Designs: Larger versions for side tables or outdoor furniture applications
  • Material Variations: Exploring wood, metal, or composite materials for different aesthetics
  • Modular Systems: Creating interchangeable components for customizable furniture sets
  • Enhanced Features: Incorporating folding mechanisms or adjustable height options
  • Sustainable Materials: Using recycled or biodegradable materials for eco-friendly production

Conclusion

The X-shaped V-leg table project successfully demonstrated how thoughtful geometric design can create stable, functional furniture from minimal components. The interlocking slot system proved to be an elegant solution that eliminates the need for additional fasteners while maintaining structural integrity. This approach to portable furniture design has broad applications across various scales and contexts, from emergency relief situations to everyday portable furniture needs.
